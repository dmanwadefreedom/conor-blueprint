# The Content Engine VSL Script

**Product:** The Content Engine (Elevated AI)
**Runtime target:** ~6:40 (about 980 spoken words at 145-155 wpm)
**Presenter:** The client-facing AI clone. That is the point. The video is its own product demo.
**Watch page:** `/content-engine/vsl.html` (this transcript is embedded there in the collapsible)
**Format:** 16:9 horizontal for the watch page. Word-level karaoke captions burned in throughout.
**Last updated:** July 2026

---

## Delivery notes for the clone build

- Pace: 145-155 wpm. Short lines. Hit the periods.
- `(pause)` = 0.6-0.9 second beat, hold eye contact, no filler.
- No music under the hook. Bring in a low, minimal bed at Beat 2 and duck it at every `(pause)`.
- The presenter never smiles through the hook. Deadpan until "that's the point."
- Numbers are spelled out the way they should be spoken. On-screen text shows the numerals.
- Cut style: hard cuts only. No transitions. B-roll cutaways 2-4 seconds max, always back to the presenter for the key line.

## Beat 2: The Problem (0:35-1:55)

**[B-ROLL: phone scrolling a feed, thumb flicking past competitor posts. Cut back to presenter on "every single day."]**

Here's the trap you're in.

You already know content is the game. Every client you'll ever sign is scrolling a feed right now, and they buy from whoever shows up in that feed every single day.

You've heard the fix a thousand times: post daily, be everywhere, build the brand.

**[B-ROLL: calendar app with two lonely posts, then nothing. An abandoned ring light in a corner.]**

So you tried. Be honest. (pause)

You filmed for one ambitious weekend. You posted for two weeks. Then a client fire broke out, and the feed went dark. Again.

**[SCENE: presenter, tighter framing. ON-SCREEN stack builds line by line: "$65K content manager / $100K video editor, loaded / $150-$250 per UGC video / ~$300K/yr in-house team"]**

Then you priced out hiring your way out of it.

A content manager runs sixty-five thousand a year. A video editor is about a hundred thousand fully loaded. UGC creators charge one-fifty to two-fifty per video. Thirty videos a month is six grand before you spend a dollar on ads.

A full in-house content team? Around three hundred thousand a year.

And here's the part nobody says out loud: (pause) even that team is too slow.

In 2026, the platforms reward volume and testing. The accounts that win aren't posting three times a week. They're testing fifty, a hundred variations a month and doubling down on what hits.

A human team can't produce at that speed.

You were never going to out-film this problem.

## Beat 3: Mechanism Reveal (1:55-3:20)

**[SCENE: presenter, medium shot. ON-SCREEN: "THE CONTENT ENGINE" in thin type, then "Part 1 / Part 2 / Part 3" appearing as each is named.]**

So we stopped trying to out-film it, and built a machine instead.

It's called the Content Engine. Three parts.

**[B-ROLL: waveform of a voice memo turning into a grid of finished posts.]**

Part one: the repurposing engine. Feed it anything: a podcast, a sales call, a voice memo from your car. It pulls out every hook, every story, every strong opinion, and turns them into weeks of finished posts. Written in your voice. Formatted for each platform.

**[SCENE: presenter gestures at themselves. This line lands hardest delivered by the clone.]**

Part two: the reel studio. This is the part you're looking at. (pause)

It writes full scripts and films them with an AI presenter: a polished stock avatar, or a broadcast-grade clone of you, face and voice. Three content paths: a storytelling pitch that lands on a call to action, a real-life use story, and a straight show and tell. B-roll cut in automatically.

Built in the two formats that actually travel right now: six-point-three-second loops viewers rewatch two or three times without noticing, and fourteen-to-sixteen-second reels tuned for completion rate, the number the algorithm cares about most.

**[B-ROLL: scheduler filling a week; a blog post publishing; a phone notification: "3 posts awaiting approval."]**

Part three: distribution. SEO blogs publish to your site on autopilot, wired so search engines and AI assistants start finding and recommending you. Every post auto-schedules to peak times on every platform.

And nothing goes live until you tap approve. (pause)

You stay editor-in-chief. You just stop being the factory.
