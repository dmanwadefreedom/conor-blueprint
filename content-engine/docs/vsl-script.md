## Delivery notes for the clone build

- Pace: 145–155 wpm. Short lines. Hit the periods.
- `(pause)` = 0.6–0.9 second beat, hold eye contact, no filler.
- No music under the hook. Bring in a low, minimal bed at Beat 2 and duck it at every `(pause)`.
- The presenter never smiles through the hook. Deadpan until "that's the point."
- Numbers are spelled out the way they should be spoken. On-screen text shows the numerals.
- Cut style: hard cuts only. No transitions. B-roll cutaways 2–4 seconds max, always back to the presenter for the key line.

## Beat 2 — The Problem (0:35–1:55)

**[B-ROLL: phone scrolling a feed, thumb flicking past competitor posts. Cut back to presenter on "every single day."]**

Here's the trap you're in.

You already know content is the game. Every client you'll ever sign is scrolling a feed right now, and they buy from whoever shows up in that feed every single day.

You've heard the fix a thousand times: post daily, be everywhere, build the brand.

**[B-ROLL: calendar app with two lonely posts, then nothing. An abandoned ring light in a corner.]**

So you tried. Be honest. (pause)

You filmed for one ambitious weekend. You posted for two weeks. Then a client fire broke out, and the feed went dark. Again.

**[SCENE: presenter, tighter framing. ON-SCREEN stack builds line by line: "$65K — content manager / $100K — video editor, loaded / $150–$250 — per UGC video / ~$300K/yr — in-house team"]**

Then you priced out hiring your way out of it.

A content manager runs sixty-five thousand a year. A video editor is about a hundred thousand fully loaded. UGC creators charge one-fifty to two-fifty per video — thirty videos a month is six grand before you spend a dollar on ads.

A full in-house content team? Around three hundred thousand a year.

And here's the part nobody says out loud: (pause) even that team is too slow.

In 2026, the platforms reward volume and testing. The accounts that win aren't posting three times a week — they're testing fifty, a hundred variations a month and doubling down on what hits.

A human team can't produce at that speed.

You were never going to out-film this problem.
