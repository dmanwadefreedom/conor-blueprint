Fair Questions
Asked Before Buying
Will it look like AI?

The litmus test is simple: does it feel native, or does it feel like an ad? The Engine is built to pass that test. Your clone is trained on your real footage and your real voice. Scripts follow the organic formats already winning your feed — storytelling, day-in-the-life, show-and-tell.

UGC-style creative outperforms polished brand content (~4x the CTR at roughly half the CPC) precisely because it doesn't look produced. And nothing publishes without your approval — if a video reads fake to you, kill it with one tap.

What about platform rules on AI content?

Handled automatically. TikTok and Meta both allow AI-generated content when it's labeled, and the Engine applies the correct label per platform. Meta has stated its AI label carries no distribution penalty.

Scripts follow policy too: AI avatars deliver demos and information — never fake personal testimonials. EU labeling requirements arriving August 2026 are already built in.

Who owns my clone and the content?

You do. Your likeness, your voice model, and every asset the Engine produces are yours, contractually. Cancel and we hand over the files and delete our copies. Your clone never appears in anyone else's content. Ever.

How much of my time does this take?

About 20 minutes a week. You open the approval queue, kill anything off-brand, and tap approve. Setup week asks the most of you: roughly 20 minutes of recorded footage and one onboarding call. By day 60, most clients approve in a single weekly batch.

What do you need from me to start?

Three things. About 20 minutes of footage — a podcast, a Zoom recording, or clips we walk you through filming on your phone. Your brand assets. Access to your social accounts.

That's the whole list. If you have existing content sitting around — episodes, blog posts, old videos — the Engine turns it into your first month of posts.

I already have an agency or an editor.

Keep them if they're working. Clients typically do one of two things: replace the $5k/mo retainer outright, or keep the strategist and let the Engine handle production at ~$25 per asset instead of $150–$300. Either way your cost per asset drops 80–90%.

What if the content doesn't perform?

Performance isn't a hope here — it's a process. Every variant is measured on 3-second hold and completion. Anything under a 25% hook rate is killed at 48 hours. Winners get scaled and refreshed every 10–14 days.

The system is built to find what works in your niche, not assume it. You see every number on the dashboard.
