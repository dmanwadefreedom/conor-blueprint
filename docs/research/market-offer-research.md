# Market & Offer Research — Pricing an "AI Ads Department" for Agencies and Mid-Size Companies (July 2026)

Research compiled July 2026. Covers what businesses currently pay for full-service paid-ads agencies, in-house creative teams, and AI UGC tools; how competitors position "AI creative department" offers; what a **$19,997 install + $1,997/mo** offer must include to beat a $5k/mo agency; agency white-label appetite; and proven high-ticket B2B funnel structures with current conversion benchmarks.

## 1. What Businesses Pay Full-Service Paid-Ads Agencies (2026)

### Retainer ranges
- **Typical PPC/paid-social management retainers: $1,500–$10,000/mo** for SMB → mid-market accounts; enterprise retainers reach **$25,000+/mo**.
- Entry retainers of **$1,000–$1,500/mo** buy campaign babysitting only — build, monitor, adjust. No meaningful creative production, no CRO, no strategy depth.
- **Local/SMB full-service marketing retainers: $2,500–$10,000+/mo** in 2026.
- **Percentage-of-ad-spend model:** realistic average is **10–20% of monthly spend** (a $20k/mo spender pays ~$2,000–$4,000/mo in management fees).
- Pricing scales with: industry competitiveness, ad spend volume, number of platforms, and whether creative production + CRO are in scope.

### What a $5,000/mo Meta/Facebook ads agency actually delivers
- Standard scope: account strategy, campaign setup, ongoing optimization, creative direction, copy, testing, tracking, and reporting — with depth and frequency varying widely between shops.
- **Creative is frequently NOT included at this price.** Many agencies charge the management fee for strategy/optimization only and bill ad creative separately at **+$500–$3,000/mo** depending on volume.
- Third-party tool costs (reporting dashboards, competitive intel, creative-testing software) are often passed through at **+$100–$500/mo**.
- Net: a mid-size company "paying $5k/mo" is realistically all-in at **$5,600–$8,500/mo** once creative and tools are added — **$67k–$100k/yr** — for an external team with other clients.

- A **minimum viable in-house marketing department ≈ $587k/yr** including salaries, benefits, tools, and turnover. Equivalent agency scope: $250k–$350k/yr.

**Anchor for the offer:** the cheapest credible in-house version of "creative + media buying" is **$250k–$600k/yr**. $19,997 + $1,997/mo = **~$44k year one, ~$24k/yr after** — 8–13% of the in-house number and roughly one-third of a $5k/mo agency's all-in cost.

## 4. How Competitors Position "AI Creative Department" Offers

- **Icon.com — "The First AI Admaker" / "The Human Admaker."** Bundles 14 ad products into one suite; "AdGPT" writes scripts/copy, "AI CMO" scans your site + competitor ads, drafts campaigns 50–99% complete, and uploads them — explicitly sold as **replacing the agency and the CMO**. Marketing literally runs "AI is coming for you" / "will replace your entire marketing team." Mid-2025 pivot added human creators back in ($399 = 6 human-filmed UGC ads) plus a "Managed" tier with a dedicated strategist — i.e., **even the loudest pure-AI player had to re-add humans + management to make it work.** They also run an agency partner kit / portfolio page, courting agencies rather than only displacing them.
- **Superside — "Your creative team's creative team."** Enterprise AI-powered creative subscription, $15k/mo minimum, annual contract; sells "creative-as-a-service" with AI workflows baked in, positioned as an ongoing partnership, not projects.
- **2026 agency-market backdrop (Ad Age, JPMorgan, Foxwell):** buyers pay premiums for **proprietary workflow/data integration that demonstrably improves outcomes**, not for "we use AI" claims. Agencies are restructuring around "AI operators" — people who orchestrate bots/automations — while "bloated creative departments become expensive relics." Specialization is winning new business.
- Boutique "AI creative studios" (Lucent Arc, Evize) sell **generative AI production + human art direction** as a hybrid — again, the human judgment layer is always the headline.
- **Positioning takeaway:** the market has already educated buyers that (a) AI can produce ad volume, (b) pure self-serve AI tools underdeliver without an operator, and (c) "install a department" framing (system + operator + accountability) is the credible middle ground. An "AI Ads Department installed inside your business" rides this wave while dodging the two failure modes: "just another tool" and "just another agency."

## 5. Benchmark: Install-Fee + Retainer Pricing in the AI-Services Market

- Standard AI-agency structure in 2026 is exactly this shape: **one-time setup/build fee + recurring retainer**, listed as separate line items.
- Typical setup fees: **$3,000–$10,000** (complex builds $2,500–$15,000+). Typical retainers: **$500–$5,000/mo** for automation upkeep; **$3,000–$8,000/mo** for AI marketing agencies running content/SEO/social workflows; **$5,000–$25,000/mo** for ongoing advisory/roadmap execution.
- Productized examples in market: "AI receptionist for dental practices, deployed in 5 days, from $1,497"; missed-call text-back systems at $1,500/mo including setup + optimization.
- **Read on $19,997 install:** it sits at the very top of the setup-fee market — 2–4x the common ceiling — so it must be justified as a *department install* (multi-system build + training + assets + launch), not an "automation setup." The $1,997/mo retainer, by contrast, is **at or below the low end** of AI-marketing retainers and ~40% of a mid-tier agency fee — that contrast ("heavy install, light retainer") is the deliberate asymmetry that makes the recurring side feel like a no-brainer.

## 6. What $19,997 + $1,997/mo Must Credibly Include to Beat a $5k/mo Agency

The comparison math the offer must make explicit:

| | $5k/mo agency (all-in) | In-house team | AI Ads Department |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year-1 cost | ~$67k–$100k | ~$300k–$587k | **~$44k** |
| Year-2+ cost | ~$67k–$100k/yr | ~$300k+/yr | **~$24k/yr** |
| Creative volume | often billed extra, 5–20 assets/mo | limited by headcount | 30–100+ AI variants/mo at ~$5–11 marginal cost |
| Ownership | agency owns process; leaves when you leave | you own it | **installed in the client's business — they keep the system** |

To be a no-brainer, the install ($19,997) must package everything an agency amortizes invisibly, delivered as owned assets:

1. **Full ad system build** — account/tracking architecture (pixel, CAPI, attribution), campaign structures, audience research, competitor ad-library teardown.
2. **Creative engine install** — AI UGC pipeline (tool stack configured under client accounts), brand-trained avatars/voices, hook/script libraries, a codified creative-testing methodology (naming conventions, kill criteria, iteration cadence).
3. **Funnel assets** — landing page(s)/VSL built and CRO'd, offer positioning, copy.
4. **Operating system** — SOPs, dashboards, weekly testing rhythm documentation, team training so the client's staff can run it ("department install," not deliverables).
5. **Launch** — first 30–60 days of campaigns actually shipped and iterated, not just handed over.

The retainer ($1,997/mo) must then cover the ongoing judgment layer that raw tools can't: fresh creative batches monthly, media buying/optimization, performance reviews, strategy iteration, and new-format adoption. At tool COGS of $150–$600/mo, margin stays 70%+.

**Framing levers the research supports:**
- Anchor against the in-house department ($300k–$600k/yr) first, agency ($67k–$100k all-in) second — both make $44k year one look trivial.
- The install fee buys **owned infrastructure** (agencies rent you theirs); the retainer is **~60% below** the agency all-in for higher creative volume.
- Buyers in 2026 pay premiums for "workflow integration that demonstrably improves outcomes" — sell the installed system + operator, never "AI tool access."
- Risk reversal matters at this price: money-back/performance guarantee messaging measurably lifts conversion (a CXL-cited test: +32% when a money-back guarantee is stated in the VSL itself).

## 7. Agency White-Label Appetite

- **~73% of agencies already resell white-label services**; the white-label market is projected around **$99B by 2026**. AI adoption among US businesses jumped from <5% (early 2024) to ~1 in 5 (2026) — agencies are racing to package AI they didn't build.
- Economics driving demand: white-label AI resold under agency brand carries **65–80% margins** (fixed platform license, no variable labor); agencies report **30–40% profit-margin lift** from white-label AI in their stack.
- Observed reseller earnings: agencies adding **$50k–$150k/yr recurring** reselling white-label AI voice agents; solo resellers with 5–10 clients at $500/mo net $500–$2k/mo; operators with 20–50 clients at $1,000/mo clear $4k–$20k/mo.
- Hot categories: AI agents/receptionists/voice, content & SEO automation, workflow automation, CRM (GoHighLevel — the model here: 66,000+ partners run on Vendasta's reselling model).
- **Gap in the market:** the established white-label plays are voice/chat/CRM — a white-label **AI ad-creative + media-buying department** is not yet commoditized. Agencies feel the exact pain (§1–2: creative production is their cost center and their most common unbundled line item), making them a natural second customer segment: license the system so they can deliver creative volume without hiring editors.
- Two-tier implication for the offer: direct-to-brand ($19,997 + $1,997) and an agency/white-label tier (multi-client license, higher retainer or per-seat) mirror how every successful platform in this space (Vendasta, GHL, Icon's partner kit) monetizes agencies.

## 8. Funnel Structure for a High-Ticket B2B Offer ($19,997 Install)

### Direct checkout vs. application/call — settled at this price point
- Consensus across 2026 sources: **you cannot sell a $7,500+ engagement with a sales page and a checkout button.** At $10k+, personal contact by phone/video is "nearly always necessary." Direct checkout is for low-ticket only.
- The recommended structure for $5k+ offers is the **VSL → application → booked call** funnel: short VSL hits pain and builds urgency → application form qualifies (budget, fit, willingness) → calendar booking → sales call.
- The application step is deliberate friction: it filters unqualified prospects *before* they consume a 45–90-minute call, and the act of applying raises commitment/perceived exclusivity.
- Sales-call mechanics for $10k+: **45–90 minutes**, with **60–70% of the call spent on discovery** before any pitch.
- Speed-to-lead dominates: responding within 5 minutes converts **8x** better; **78% of buyers go with the first vendor to respond.**

### Recommended funnel shape (supported by benchmarks below)
Paid ad (short-form, organic-style — see short-form research doc) → VSL page → application → calendar (Calendly-style) → confirmation/indoctrination assets (case study, "what to expect" video) → call → close. A low-friction "watch the breakdown" retargeting layer catches non-appliers.

| Visitor → lead (B2B median) | 2.9% | organic 2.7%, email 2.4%, paid social 0.9% |

### Unit-economics sketch (using mid-range benchmarks)
1,000 paid visitors → ~3% apply (30 applications) → ~45% book (13–14 calls) → ~70% show → ~15–20% close ≈ **1.5–2 clients per 1,000 visitors**. At $19,997 install + $1,997/mo, one close covers ~$10k–$20k of traffic cost — the offer supports aggressive CAC, which is itself a selling point when pitching the system to clients.

### VSL specifics for 2026
- Short-form VSLs are the top performer for cold traffic; hold attention past the 90-second cliff or cut shorter.
- Optimize mobile-first (70%+ of VSL views are on phones).
- State the guarantee inside the video (+32% conversion in CXL-cited testing).
- Manage to **revenue per view**, not watch rate alone; rising CPMs mean a funnel that converted at 3% in 2022 needs ~4% in 2026 to stay profitable.

## 9. Bottom Line for the Offer

1. **Price positioning is sound:** $44k year one sits between "AI tool" ($600–$6k/yr, no results) and "$5k/mo agency" ($67k–$100k/yr all-in) — and at 8–13% of an in-house department. Anchor high (in-house), compare mid (agency), dismiss low (tools).
2. **The install must be framed as owned infrastructure** — system + assets + trained team stay with the client. This is the single sharpest differentiator vs. agencies (rented) and tools (empty).
3. **The retainer is deliberately underpriced vs. market** ($1,997 vs $3k–$8k AI-marketing retainers) — margin holds at 70%+ because tool COGS is $150–$600/mo.
4. **Sell it via VSL → application → call. No direct checkout at $19,997.** Budget for 45–90-min discovery-heavy calls; 30–60% application→call, 10–25% call→close are the planning numbers.
5. **Agency white-label is a validated expansion path** (73% of agencies resell, 65–80% margins) and creative production is precisely the line item agencies unbundle today — but the direct-to-brand offer should prove the system first.
