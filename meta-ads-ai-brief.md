## 1. Core operating principles

1. **Psychology beats media buying.** Platform mechanics change constantly; human psychology doesn't. An amateur media buyer with broad targeting and a killer ad beats the world's best media buyer with a boring ad. Never obsess over account structure or targeting hacks.
2. **You are not selling the product — you are selling the click.** The ad's only job is to funnel qualified people to the landing page. The page/funnel sells.
3. **The market awareness pyramid:** only ~3% of the market is ready to buy now, 17% is information-gathering, 20% is problem-aware, 60% is unaware. Meta ads work across the whole pyramid — call out problems, warm people up, educate them until they're ready to buy.
4. **Your competition is not other businesses — it's Netflix, TMZ, UFC and everything else in the feed.** The ad must flood the prospect's brain with dopamine or it loses the attention war.
5. **The ad has exactly two jobs:** (a) deliver news, value, or insight; (b) sell the click with curiosity.
6. **Don't hate clickbait — engineer it responsibly.** Pure blind clickbait = bad experience. Pure benefit with no intrigue = ignored. The winning ad sits at the intersection: **burning curiosity + a big, specific, targeted benefit = the Hyper-Dopamine Ad.**

## 2. Model the top of the feed, not other advertisers

The most-viewed pages on Facebook (per Meta's own Widely Viewed Content Report) are news/gossip publishers: LADbible, UNILAD, E! News, People, TMZ. People are on the platform for news and gossip — ads should mimic that native style.

**Headline transposition method** — take a proven publisher headline pattern and swap in your niche:

| Publisher pattern | Transposed for a business |
|---|---|
| "Taylor Swift fans lose it after spotting X…" | "Australians are losing it over this home-loan hack" (mortgage broker) |
| "iPhone users should do this to save battery" | "CEOs are being told to do this one thing before driving home to their family" (exec coach) |
| "Insane new documentary leaves viewers speechless" | "Insane new mindset trick leaves anxiety sufferers speechless" (anxiety coach) |
| "Miami mom discovers mysterious metabolism booster" | (weight loss for pre/post-menopausal women) |
| "REVEALED: …" / "Breaking:" / "…finally breaks his silence" | "REVEALED: This secret Victorian suburb is set to boom in 2026" (property investing) |

Rule: **never write ads from a blank page.** Pull live inspiration from LADbible, UNILAD, TMZ, E! Online, People, tabloid magazine covers, then adapt.

- **Blind curiosity (bad):** "You won't believe what Ivanka Trump does every morning" — no benefit, attracts everyone, teaches the algorithm nothing.
- **Curiosity + targeted benefit (good):** "Supermodels use these 3 secret tricks to look years younger" — only attracts people who want the benefit, and trains the algorithm on who to find.

## 3. Anatomy of a Hyper-Dopamine Ad (build every ad in this order)

1. **Scroll-stopping image (pattern interrupt).** Weird, native-looking, provokes "what is going on here?" Works well: primary image + a secondary inset image (LADbible style), red circles/arrows, odd objects (e.g. holding a USB stick on a black background), real people, multiple faces (the algorithm favors it).
2. **Eye-catching headline.** Curiosity + specific benefit. E.g. "This client-acquisition hack feels almost illegal."
3. **Slippery intro (first line of primary text).** The most overlooked element — changing only the intro has produced 5x ROAS / 5x lower CPL. It must re-stop the scroller and pull them into the body.
4. **Butter body copy.** Long-form primary text. Keep to ≤2,200 characters so one asset runs across Facebook + Instagram placements. Write freely first, then cut aggressively.
5. **Fascinating link description.** Also overlooked. E.g. "Look closely, then click."
6. **Non-threatening CTA button.** **"Learn More" wins split tests.** Never "Buy Now" / "Apply Now" / "Sign Up" at the top of funnel.

### Hyper-Dopamine checklist (every ad must pass all five)
- [ ] Does the creative interrupt the pattern / look weird enough to stop the scroll?
- [ ] Does the creative itself generate curiosity?
- [ ] Does the headline contain burning curiosity?
- [ ] Is there a big, **specific** benefit for the target market?
- [ ] Does it look like native content, NOT an ad?

## 4. Copywriting rules

- **Readability: grade 3–5 level.** Run copy through a Hemingway-style readability check. Simpler = more readers = more buyers. Consumption drives conversion.
- **Short words, short sentences, short paragraphs.** One- and two-syllable words. Lots of line breaks — 80–90% read on mobile; walls of text get skipped like long emails.
- **Write like you talk.** No fancy words, no "brand voice" self-congratulation. Nobody cares about the brand — only "what's in it for me."
- **Write to one person, not a group.** Conversational and direct — but **avoid literal "you/your" phrasing that calls out personal attributes** (weight, finances, health), which Meta rejects. Reframe: not "Sabri wants tacos" → "Let's go get tacos. I'm buying."
- **Specificity = believability.** Never "lose weight" → "lose 12–19 kg in the next 5 weeks." Never "more leads" → "90–480 inbound leads every single month." Vague claims breed doubt.
- **Positive angle beats pain avoidance ~8 times out of 10.** "Lose 20–50 lbs with this one weird hack" beats "Don't gain 50 lbs this winter." Test both, expect the positive gain-frame to win.
- **Long copy beats short copy — IF it's interesting.** Who reads all that text? The buyers. Boring short copy is just as dead as boring long copy.
- **Deliver real value in the ad itself** (news, insight, entertainment) so people comment, share, and click — you're writing for humans AND for the engagement signals the machine rewards. High engagement → better inventory and lower CPMs from Meta.
- **Once you find a winning angle, align everything to it:** winning headline → matching creative → matching intro → matching body. Milk one winning angle fully before moving on.
- Proof, credibility, scarcity, urgency, risk-reversal all matter — but they are secondary to **hitting the market's bullseye desire.** When unsure what the market wants, run multiple angles head-to-head and let performance answer.

## 5. Proven creative formats (rotate a healthy mix)

1. **Raw Native** — iPhone-quality photo, looks like an organic post (e.g. casual photo of founder + spouse). The #1 rule of advertising: ads must not look like ads. People hate ads; hundreds of millions run ad blockers.
2. **Breaking News** — the single most reliable format across every niche ("Shark Tank investor drops client-acquisition bombshell", "…finally breaks his silence"). If forced to bet on one format, bet on this — but never run it exclusively.
3. **SMS / text-message screenshot** — a fake-native text conversation; customize sender and message to the niche (a house, a bank statement, whatever's relevant).
4. **Native Highlight** — native photo + optional second image marked with a red circle and arrow. Simple, extremely effective.
5. **Native Post** — styled like a LADbible/TMZ feed post with a small secondary image ("Notorious 'Internet Daddy' shares 8 viral client-getting hacks").
6. **Secret Intel** — map/document-style reveal ("REVEALED: this secret suburb will boom…"). Swap in insight from your own content offer.

Favorites that work in every market: **Raw Native + Breaking News.**

## 6. The eight scaling hacks (2026 playbook)

### Hack 1 — Statics beat video
Since Meta's **Andromeda** update, the algorithm needs an absurd volume of fresh creative to avoid fatigue. Video (VSLs, UGC) is too slow/expensive to produce at that volume; thousands of static variations can be made cheaply and fast. Meta's algorithm is also **biased toward statics** (it can serve more of them per session, which maximizes Meta's revenue). Down-and-dirty native statics routinely outperform VSLs that took months to produce.
**Action:** block one hour per week, every week, dedicated to producing fresh static creatives for the winning offer.

### Hack 2 — The one-keyword (identity trigger) hack
Andromeda reads your image, copy, offer, and landing page to decide who to serve. To reach a new pocket of audience, put that audience **in the creative and copy**. Take the winning ad, duplicate it, and insert a niche/demographic keyword:
- "How to get 462 leads per week on autopilot" → "How to get 462 **dental** leads…", "…**lawyer** leads…", "…**property-investment** leads…"
Base the keyword list on the 20% of clients that drive 80% of revenue. This drops cost per lead dramatically and opens audience pockets a generic ad can't reach.

### Hack 3 — The winning-format clone (+ zombie campaign)
Never ride one winning ad until it fatigues and dies. Instead:
1. Feed the winning ad to an AI with this framing: *"Read this ad and become its author. If we showed the next ad you write to 100 people, not one in 100 could tell it was written by a different person."*
2. Generate hundreds of variations: "now write it for a 25-year-old woman", "for a 30-year-old man", etc. Same voice, different resonance points.
3. Order of operations: body copy variations first → then headlines → then creative.
4. Put all variants in a **CBO** and let Meta shift spend to winners.
5. **Zombie campaign:** ads that got zero spend but that you still believe in — pull them into their own fresh ad set and relaunch. Typically recovers another ~20% of winners.

### Hack 4 — Don't make ads look like ads
Run what's already proven to work organically. If a piece of organic content has big view counts, move it to paid and pour gasoline on it. No viral content of your own? Create a **burner account** (fresh TikTok/IG), follow every influencer/page in the niche, engage with their content — the algorithm will surface the niche's best-performing content styles. Clone those styles as paid ads.

### Hack 5 — Broad targeting + hyper-specific creative
**The creative IS the targeting now.** Skip interest stacking. Give Meta a huge context window: long-form copy that describes exactly who the ad is for, then run it broad (country-level only). Don't try to out-target Meta's engineers — spend the energy on creative instead.
**Test:** duplicate the best interest-stacked ad, strip targeting to country-only, run both 7 days, compare CPA.

### Hack 6 — Ad ↔ landing page congruence (scent match)
Most advertisers put 90% of effort into ads and 10% into the page. If the page doesn't match what was clicked, people bounce. Meta is the best split-testing tool on Earth — headlines get ~1,000x more impressions than the landing page gets visits. Run 20–40 headline variants, find the winner, then **mirror the winning ad headline in the page's headline, sub-headline, and lead-in.** Minimum expected lift: 15–20% conversion rate. Keep **at least three split tests running at all times.**
**Action:** compare the top 3 ads' headlines against their landing pages side by side. If incongruent, fix within 24 hours.

### Hack 7 — Retarget with a DIFFERENT offer
The #1 reason someone didn't buy is the offer wasn't right for them — so don't just re-serve the same ad. Retargeting stack:
1. **Objection-handling ads** — built from real reasons prospects didn't book/buy (gathered by outbound-calling every lead who downloads the free report).
2. **Proof/testimonial carousel ads** — evidence the claims are real.
3. **Different-offer CBO** — best ads for every other product/service, retargeted to non-buyers (e.g. protein-powder clickers retargeted with creatine can jump ROAS from 2–3x to 6x).
4. **Value-framed audit/call offer** — dress the sales call up as a standalone piece of value the prospect keeps regardless of buying.

### Hack 8 — Track the metric that actually matters
Optimize **blended ROAS at the business level and net free cash flow**, not ad-account ROAS percentage. Find break-even ROAS / max allowable cost to acquire a customer, and scale spend toward it aggressively — a ROAS drop from 10 to 5 while going from $1K/mo to $100K/mo spend means far more net cash. Data → confidence → speed and conviction.
**Action:** block 3 hours per month to personally verify the numbers (not the analyst's summary, not the media buyer's report).

## 7. Storytelling layer (for video scripts, VSLs, and long-form ads)

People buy on **emotion, not logic**. Four pillars for any story-driven ad or VSL:
1. **Grab attention** via (a) surprise/pattern break, (b) emotion, (c) "calling them by their name" — content so relatable the viewer feels personally addressed (like "Just Do It" feels aimed at you). All within the first 10 seconds.
2. **Make them feel something** — get content, character, and circumstances all working at once.
3. **Teach them something** — real value, not empty calories; people lean in when they're learning inside a story.
4. **Leave them wanting more** — plant a question the audience needs answered (the curiosity gap). Master storytellers are defined by **what they leave out**: include details that move the story forward, withhold details that make people lean in and ask.

## 8. Standing test-and-scale protocol (give this to the AI as its default loop)

1. Generate ads using the Hyper-Dopamine anatomy (Section 3) across multiple formats (Section 5) and multiple angles/hypotheses about market desire.
2. Launch broad (country-only targeting), statics-first, in a CBO.
3. Read results in ~7 days: keep winners by CPA/CPL and CTR, not vanity metrics.
4. Clone winners: keyword/niche variants (Hack 2), authored-voice variants (Hack 3), zombie-revive unspent ads.
5. Mirror winning headlines onto landing pages (Hack 6); keep ≥3 split tests live.
6. Build the retargeting stack (Hack 7).
7. Scale spend toward break-even ROAS on blended, business-level numbers (Hack 8).
8. Refresh creative weekly, forever. Fatigue is the default; volume is the antidote.

---

## 9. Quick reference — do / don't

**DO**
- Sell the click, not the product
- Curiosity + specific benefit in every headline
- Native-looking, weird, pattern-interrupting creative
- Long, simple, line-broken, grade 3–5 copy
- "Learn More" CTA
- Statics at volume; broad targeting; specific creative
- Mirror ad headline on the landing page
- Retarget with different offers
- Scale to break-even blended ROAS / net cash

**DON'T**
- Make ads look like ads
- Use blind clickbait with no benefit
- Interest-stack or over-engineer targeting
- Ride a single winning ad until it dies
- Use vague claims ("get more leads", "lose weight")
- Use "Buy Now"-style CTAs cold
- Optimize for ad-account ROAS percentage over net cash flow
- Write from a blank page — always model proven headlines
