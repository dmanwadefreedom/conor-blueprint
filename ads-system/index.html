What is an AI marketing department?

For the parts an agency actually does, campaign structure, budget moves, creative testing, reporting: yes, and faster. An agency ships 15–20 assets a month and checks budgets weekly at best. GIZMO tests 100+ variants per angle, kills losers at 48 hours, and moves budget daily.

What it doesn't replace: your judgment. You set the goals and approve the direction. The department executes at machine speed underneath you. And this shift isn't hypothetical. You have about a year before agentic ad departments are normal.

Angle selection, audience strategy per platform, testing plans, budget allocation, and creative refresh cycles. Every decision is data-backed and explained in your daily report: what moved, why, and what happens next. If you'd fire a human CMO for making a call without showing their reasoning, you'll like this one.

Because it's a department build, not an account setup. Four weeks of work: full audit, pixel and tracking architecture, creative engine wiring, funnel assets, campaign builds, SOPs, team training, and a supervised launch. Typical AI-agency setup fees run $3k–$10k for a fraction of that scope.

Everything. Ad accounts, pixel data, tracking architecture, creative library, naming conventions, SOPs, and documentation, all built in your properties from day one. Cancel operations and it all keeps working. That's the opposite of the agency model, and it's on purpose.
