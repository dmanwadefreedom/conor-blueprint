## 0. Campaign thesis

The product demos itself. Every asset in this campaign is the AI Ads Department doing the thing we sell: the clone presenting is the creative engine's output, the screen captures are GIZMO actually running campaigns, and the swarm volume is the 100-variant testing matrix pointed at our own offer. GIZMO sells GIZMO. Nothing in this campaign should be producible by a company that doesn't own the system.

**Core proof asset:** a live install is running a real estate operation's ads right now. That sentence, at that level of specificity, is the ceiling. Never the client's name, market, or numbers.

**Primary conversion event:** install call booked (`cal.com/REPLACE_ELEVATED_AI/install-call`).
**Secondary:** VSL watch (`ads-system/vsl.html`), which triggers Sequence 2 in `ads-system/docs/email-sequences.md`.

## 1. The campaign spine: the one-year window

Every channel tells the same four-act story. Assets get made in all four acts from day one, but the weighting shifts across the month. This is the narrative skeleton every video, email, and ad hangs on — not a list of slogans to rotate randomly.

| Act | Narrative beat | Anchor hook | Funnel job | Weight by week |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1. It's here | Agentic ad departments exist and run today. This is not a demo reel, it's a live system. | "Fire your ads agency. Agentic Ad Departments are here." | Scroll-stop, awareness, pool-building | W1 60% → W4 30% |
| 2. It runs | Watch it work. Dashboards, budget moves, kill decisions, the daily report. Proof over pitch. | "The entire ads agency, run from your phone." | Consideration, VSL clicks | W1 30% → W4 30% |
| 3. The window | This is where early adopters are right now. In about a year it's table stakes and the arbitrage is gone. | "You have about a year before this is normal." | Urgency, retargeting pressure | W1 10% → W4 25% |
| 4. Pick a side | For agencies specifically: adopt it or lose clients to someone who did. | "Have your agency use it now, or be tossed aside by someone who does." | Agency-segment conversion, white-label pipeline | W1 0% → W4 15% |

**Why this works as urgency:** it is honest. We are not claiming scarcity of the product; we are describing the adoption curve of a technology. "About a year before this is normal" costs us nothing legally (it's a market prediction, not a results promise) and reframes procrastination as competitive risk — the strongest lever on a B2B buyer who is currently comfortable. The one operational scarcity claim we make anywhere is "four installs a month," and only because it's true (see guardrails).

**How it lands per audience:**
- **In-house founder/CMO:** the window means "your competitor installs this before you do." Their ads get cheaper and faster; yours stay on the agency treadmill.
- **Agency owner:** the window means "your clients hear 'fire your ads agency' before you have an answer." The white-label license IS the answer.

## 2. Channel 1: the AI influencer swarm

**Objective:** 50–100 videos/day live by Week 4, showing the system running, feeding the retargeting pool, and surfacing organic winners to convert into paid creative. This is the Content Engine's 100-video testing matrix pointed at our own B2B offer — the campaign is the case study.

### 2.1 What the videos are

Two raw ingredient types, recombined by the variant matrix:

1. **Screen capture of GIZMO working.** Real dashboards, real campaign actions: the morning CMO report rendering, a variant getting killed at the 48-hour mark, budget moving to a winner, the creative queue filling, the approval queue on a phone. Rules: capture from the live real estate install ONLY with all client-identifying data redacted (account names, campaign names, geo, spend figures that could identify) — or capture from our own GIZMO instance running this launch campaign, which needs no redaction and is the preferred source. Never show revenue, ROAS, lead counts, or CPA figures as implied client results; showing *system actions* (kills, scales, reports, queue) is the point, not performance numbers.
2. **Clone talking-head reactions.** The same clone presenter as the VSL, reacting to / narrating the screen captures. Because the presenter is our own AI clone speaking about our own product, it is a brand spokesperson, not an influencer endorsement — but it still must be scripted as **demonstration and information, never testimonial** ("here's what it's doing" — never "I tried this and it made me money"). This keeps us inside TikTok's AI-influencer policy (virtual humans may state factual product info; they may not deliver personal endorsements) and inside FTC lines simultaneously.

**Formats** (per `docs/research/short-form-best-practices-2026.md`):
- **6.3s seamless loops** — split-sentence edit, continuous audio bed, one system action per loop ("watch GIZMO kill a losing ad" — the kill lands at the loop point). Reach/awareness plays.
- **14–16s reels** — hook, one micro-story or demo beat, one CTA. The completion-rate sweet spot; the conversion-leaning organic unit.
- **30–45s Shorts cuts** — YouTube Shorts over-indexes slightly longer; same ingredients, one extra beat of explanation. Human-varied scripts per video (see YouTube policy note in 2.5).

### 2.2 The three content paths, applied to a B2B offer

Every video maps to exactly one path. Each path gets its own hook-variant matrix and its own testing slots.

**Path A — Storytelling pitch → CTA** (clone talking head, no dashboard until the reveal)
- Spine: the two-invoices audit from the VSL, compressed. PAS structure: name the $5k retainer + $8k creative payroll pain → agitate ($156k/yr for 15–20 assets and a report you don't read) → reveal the department → one CTA to the VSL.
- Hooks: contrarian claim ("Fire your ads agency"), mistake warning ("Stop paying two invoices for one department"), list tease ("Three things your agency hopes you never add up").
- Length 15–35s. CTA in final 3s + pinned comment. Direct-response weight.

**Path B — Real-life use (lifestyle-native, the ad that doesn't know it's an ad)**
- Spine: a normal workday where the ads department is just... a Slack channel and a four-minute phone scroll. POV framing: "POV: your entire ads department reports before your coffee." Morning report lands, thumb scrolls the approval queue, approve-approve-kill-approve, phone goes back in pocket.
- Minimal or no pitch. Caption + pinned comment carry the CTA. 6–15s, built to loop.
- Job: reach, trust, and feeding the retargeting pool. This path makes the other two paths' claims believable.

**Path C — Show-and-tell (demo-native)**
- Spine: dashboard on screen from frame 1. "Watch what happens to an ad with a 19% hook rate at hour 48." The kill/scale decision IS the hook. Before/after of a testing matrix filling with variants. The daily CMO report scrolling with the clone narrating what each line means.
- Explicit feature-to-benefit narration, one direct CTA. 15–30s.
- Job: conversion. This is the path that books calls, because a demo removes doubt.

**Portfolio:** run all three simultaneously, roughly 30% A / 30% B / 40% C at full volume (C over-weighted because a B2B considered purchase leans on doubt-removal). Sprints of 10–20 variants, one variable isolated per sprint (hook, act of the spine, path, format length, CTA).

### 2.3 Account architecture + warm-up

Hub-and-spoke, per platform:

- **Hub:** the Elevated AI brand account on TikTok, IG, and YouTube. Gets the best 2–4 videos/day, the VSL link in bio, and all paid amplification (Spark codes pull from here first).
- **Spokes:** 15–25 themed swarm accounts across TikTok (10–14), IG (4–6), and YouTube Shorts (2–4). Each spoke is a distinct editorial persona in the niche — e.g., "agentic marketing watch," "AI ops desk," "the CMO feed," "agency obituaries" — not 25 clones of the brand account. Spokes cross-pollinate: they can react to and stitch hub content.

Non-negotiables from the research doc:
- **Unique fingerprints per account:** unique bios, avatars, posting schedules, content mixes. No shared devices or IPs — identical fingerprints get banned in batches. Budget for device/IP isolation tooling from day one.
- **14-day warm-up per account, no exceptions:** days 1–2 profile setup + feed training in the niche; days 3–5 niche browsing + light engagement; days 6–10 one low-pressure post/day; days 11–14 two-format/posting-window testing, then ramp. A fresh account posting polished volume on day 1 pattern-matches to a bot operation and the label sticks.
- **Stagger cohorts** so warm-up doesn't gate the ramp: Cohort 1 (hub + 6 spokes) starts warming in GTM Week 0; Cohort 2 (8 spokes) starts Week 1; Cohort 3 (remainder) starts Week 2.
- **AI labeling everywhere:** self-apply Meta's AI-generated label on qualifying Reels (and adopt the "AI creator" account label on spokes — Meta states it doesn't affect distribution); TikTok synthetic-media labels on every clone video (auto-detection via C2PA will catch it anyway; self-label first); YouTube "altered or synthetic content" toggle on every Short. EU AI Act Article 50 makes labeling mandatory in EU markets Aug 2, 2026 — we will already be compliant.

### 2.4 Daily quota ramp

Per-account cadence caps (from platform research: TikTok tolerates 3–5/day on a warmed account; IG 1–2/day; Shorts 1/day quality) — the swarm hits volume through account count, not per-account spam.

| Week | Accounts posting (warmed) | Videos/day | Sprint focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Hub + Cohort 1 (7) | **10/day** | One variable: hook family, within Path A vs C. Establish baseline hook rates. |
| 2 | + Cohort 2 partial (12–15) | **25/day** | Winning hook family × all three paths. First Spark authorizations collected. |
| 3 | + Cohort 2 full (18–20) | **40–60/day** | Winning path/hook combos × format length (6.3s vs 14–16s) × spine act. First paid boosts live. |
| 4 | All cohorts (22–25) | **50–100/day** | Full matrix. Iterate winners (new hook on winning body, new body under winning hook). Never just clone a winner. |

Volume math check at Week 4: 12 TikTok spokes × 4/day + hub × 4 + 5 IG × 2 + 3 Shorts × 1 ≈ 65/day floor; pushing TikTok spokes to 5/day and adding carousel/photo-mode variants clears 100. If account attrition (bans/flags) drops capacity below plan, backfill from the next warming cohort — never shortcut warm-up to plug a gap.

### 2.5 KPI thresholds: kill / hold / scale

| Signal | Threshold | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 3-sec hook rate at 48h | **< 25%** | Kill the variant. No appeals. |
| 3-sec hook rate | **> 60%** | Distribution-expansion zone: mark as candidate, iterate 3 children off it next sprint. |
| Completion rate (TikTok) | **> 70%** | Viral-threshold zone: flag for Spark boost. |
| Completion (IG 14–16s) | **> 50–60%** + replays | Flag for boost. |
| Saves + shares + comment quality | Top decile of sprint | Tiebreaker for boost selection. |
| Organic winner boosted to paid | Clears organic benchmarks above | Spark Ads (TikTok) / partnership ads (Meta) with **365-day authorization collected up front**. |
| Paid budget scaling | Winner holding target CPL | **+20–30% every 3–4 days.** Never double overnight; it resets learning. |
| Creative refresh | Every **10–14 days** (TikTok) / **14–21 days** (Meta) | Rotate fresh variants in before fatigue, from the matrix, not from scratch. |
| Account health | Views flatline across ALL content on one account for 5+ days | Rest the account 1 week at engagement-only; investigate fingerprint issues. |

**Winners → paid pipeline:** organic winner identified → Spark code / whitelisting auth pulled → boosted at equal budget across 3–5 finalists → losers killed at 48–72h → winner budgets scaled 20–30% every 3–4 days → winner's structure fed back into the matrix as a new parent. Paid runs from OUR GIZMO install — every media-buying decision in the launch account is itself screen-capture material for next week's Path C videos. Close the loop on purpose.

**YouTube-specific caution:** Shorts volume runs into the "inauthentic content" policy fastest. Shorts get human-rewritten scripts per video, original narration variance, and the synthetic-content disclosure toggled — 3–5/week per channel, quality over quota. TikTok and IG carry the volume; YouTube carries authority.

### 3.4 List and volume

- Sources: verified B2B lists only (founders/CMOs at companies with visible paid-ad footprints; agency owners via directories and LinkedIn). No scraped consumer data.

- Everyone who clicks but doesn't book flows into the retargeting pool (Section 4) via site pixel.

## 4. Channel 3: the retargeting pool

The swarm's real output in month one is not viral hits; it's a pool of people who watched an AI run an ad account. The pool is the asset. Build it deliberately:

**Pool segments (build in Meta + TikTok from day one; pixel + CAPI already firing per GTM Week 0):**

| Segment | Source | Retargeting job |
|---|---|---|
| Video engagers | 3s+ / 25% / 75% viewers of boosted swarm content, per platform | Act 2 → Act 3 creative ("you watched it run; here's the window") |
| Profile engagers | IG/TikTok account engagement audiences (hub + spokes) | Same as above; cheap to re-reach |
| Site visitors: `ads-system/index.html` | Pixel | Mid-intent: VSL push |
| VSL page visitors + 50%/95% watch depth | Pixel + player events on `ads-system/vsl.html` | High-intent: install-call push, guarantee-explainer creative, mirror email Sequence 2 |
| Pricing-anchor visitors (`index.html#pricing`) | Pixel | Highest-intent: capacity framing ("four installs a month, booking order") |
| Email clickers, no booking | ESP → custom audience sync | Cross-channel echo of Sequence 2 beats |
| Call booked / customers | CRM sync | EXCLUDE from acquisition; seed lookalikes |

**Rules:**
- Retargeting windows: 7-day hot (VSL 50%+, pricing visitors), 30-day warm (site + video engagers), 90-day cool-down archive.
- Ladder the creative to the spine: cold sees Act 1–2, pool sees Act 3, agency-tagged pool sees Act 4. Nobody should see urgency creative before proof creative.
- Frequency cap ~2/day per platform on the hot pool; this is a considered purchase, not an impulse buy.
- Lookalikes only after a seed segment passes ~1,000 members; before that, spend goes to broad + creative-volume targeting (the variant matrix IS the targeting).
- Every retargeting ad is a proven organic winner or its iteration. No net-new untested creative in retargeting.

## 5. Paid media plan (minimal, winner-fed)

- **Budget posture:** organic-first. Weeks 1–2: $0–50/day, boost-testing only. Weeks 3–4: $150–300/day split ~60% retargeting pool / 40% Spark-boosted winner scaling. All spend runs through our own GIZMO install.
- **Whitelisting hygiene:** collect Spark / partnership-ad authorizations (365-day) the moment a spoke account posts anything with winner potential, not after it wins.
- **Every boosted post carries the paid-partnership/ad disclosure the platform requires.** Native-feeling creative does not exempt us from disclosing; FTC native-ad guidance is explicit and per-post penalties are $53,088.

## 6. FTC + platform guardrails (the box)

> ### GUARDRAILS — every asset checks against this box before it ships
>
> **Claims:**
> 1. **No income, revenue, ROAS, close-rate, lead-count, or "results" claims. Ever.** Not spoken, not on-screen, not implied by a dashboard screenshot. Cost-comparison math is the only money math allowed: what a stack costs now ($5k agency + $8k creative = $156k/yr) vs. what the system costs ($19,997 + $1,997/mo; $43,961 year one). Spend you stop spending, never money you make.
> 2. **No install-count claims until true.** The reserved install-count claim (see the DO-NOT-USE-UNTIL-TRUE comment at the top of this file) and any variant of it stays out of every asset until it is factually true. The only proof claim in market: "a live install is running a real estate operation's ads right now." No client names, no market position, no client numbers, even redacted-adjacent.
> 3. **No fabricated testimonials, reviews, stats, or case studies.** "Pilot" and "live install" framing only. When real client results exist AND the client consents in writing, they still go through legal review before use.
> 4. **Guarantee wording, exactly three points, nothing more:** delivered as scoped or we keep working at no extra charge; 30-day money-back window on the install fee after launch; operations month to month and the client keeps the system. Never "risk-free," never tied to performance.
> 5. **Scarcity:** "four installs a month" only while it is operationally true. No countdown timers, no fake deadlines. "About a year before this is normal" is a market prediction and stays one; never convert it into a countdown.
>
> **AI + disclosure:**
> 6. **The clone never gives a testimonial or personal endorsement.** It demonstrates and informs ("here is what the system is doing"), never "I used this and it worked for me." This is a TikTok policy line AND an FTC line.
> 7. **Label synthetic media on every platform:** TikTok AI label (C2PA will auto-detect anyway), Meta AI-generated label + AI-creator account label on spokes, YouTube altered/synthetic toggle. EU delivery: mandatory under AI Act Art. 50 from Aug 2, 2026.
> 8. **Ads disclose as ads.** Every boosted/Spark/whitelisted post carries the platform's paid disclosure, obvious in-video, not buried in caption folds.
>
> **Data hygiene:**
> 9. Screen captures show system actions only; all client-identifying data redacted; prefer captures from our own launch install. Cold email: verified B2B contacts, working unsubscribe from email one, CAN-SPAM footer everywhere.
>
> **Escalation:** if an asset needs a claim this box doesn't allow, the asset is wrong, not the box. Kill the asset.

## 7. Measurement and weekly operating rhythm

**North star: install calls booked.** Everything else is diagnostic.

| Metric | Owner | Target by Week 4 |
|---|---|---|
| Videos published/day | Swarm op | 50–100 |
| Blended 3-sec hook rate | Swarm op | ≥ 35% (kill floor 25%) |
| Organic winners promoted to paid | Swarm op | 3–5/week |
| Retargeting pool size (all segments) | Media | 5,000+ |
| VSL unique views / avg watch depth | Media | trend up weekly; 50%+ depth on 30% of views |
| Cold email reply rate / VSL CTR | Founder | ≥ 3% reply on best cell |
| Install calls booked | Founder | 2–4/week |
| Installs closed | Founder | per GTM: 3–5 in month one |

**Weekly rhythm:** Monday sprint plan (pick the one variable this sprint isolates) → daily kill pass at 48h marks → Thursday boost decisions (Spark winners into paid) → Friday retro: what did this sprint's variable teach, which winner gets iterated, next sprint's variable. Every cycle answers one question or it was wasted volume.

**Kill criteria for the campaign itself:** if by end of Week 3 the swarm has produced zero variants above a 60% 3-sec hold AND cold email is under 1% reply across all cells, the offer framing is wrong, not the machine — stop scaling volume and re-run the Act 1 hook matrix against new pain framings before spending another week.
